## What to Look For in a UK VPS Provider

Before diving into specific plans, here are the metrics that actually separate good providers from mediocre ones:

**CPU Clock Speed vs. Core Count** — Most people default to looking at core count, but for typical web apps, CMS platforms, Discord bots, and light databases, single-core speed matters more. A 6.0 GHz single core will outperform a sluggish 2.3 GHz quad-core for the workloads most people actually run.

**Network Connectivity** — Does the UK data center peer with LINX? What Tier 1 ISPs do they connect with? This determines whether your server actually feels fast or just looks good on paper.

**Transparent Pricing** — Some providers advertise one price and then add bandwidth overages, IP fees, or "CPU burst" charges that make your actual bill unrecognizable. The number you see should be the number you pay.

**Backup Policy** — Weekly offsite automated backups should be standard. Any provider charging extra for basic backup functionality is quietly extracting money from you.

**Deployment Speed** — In 2026, waiting 24 hours for a VPS to spin up is absurd. Anything over 5 minutes is a bad sign.
